Vizo News.

 

Company: Vizo inc.
Roles: User Research, UI & UX Design, User Testing

 
 

The Project.

 

Vizo approached us with the goal to curate news for user’s to easily read on the go.

 

The Task.

 

Vizo isn’t just another aggregator nor a save-for-later. We set forth to develop a platform in which users can stay informed with all of today’s current event’s from all major publications in just five minutes.

An initial design point established was that news shouldn’t be about overdramatic headlines or link bait; we capture readers interest with a single tag/keyword, a unique image identifying the story, and a 600 character summary.

After conducting in depth user research, we developed a criteria based on five major points in which to design the app:

Urgency: News aggregators and read-laters result in a pile of unread articles. Our goal is to highlight key points of each story within 600 characters.

Uniqueness: Algorithms aren’t personal; people are personal. Each story is researched and tailored by a team of editors for the user’s consumption.

Usefulness: Vizo deliver’s the most relevant aspects of a given story in a succinct manner, allowing the user to read further from a publication of their choice.

Specificity: Inform users of current event’s within 5 minutes.

User-friendliness: The interface should allow user’s to navigate stories and topics with ease and share on any major social network.

Context: The user should be able to access and read the news in a hurry or in a relaxed state.

 

Your World at A Glance.

 
 

Glances are the core of Vizo; they deliver an image-based headline and story summary. The experience had to be seamless and easy to use as this is where users will spend majority of their time.
